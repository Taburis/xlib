Mixture Model

Definition#

A finite mixture model assumes that the observation represented by the random variables XXX is draw from a distribution that composed by KKK sub-distributions (component). To formulate the mixture model, define the following variables:

  1. Zj,j=1,…,KZ_j,j=1,\dots, KZj​,j=1,…,K is the latent variables can taken values zj=0,1z_j=0,1zj​=0,1 represents the data comes from the jjj-th component.
  2. Xi,i=1,…,NX_i,i=1,\dots, NXi​,i=1,…,N stands for the observations from the model where NNN is the sample size.
  3. θj,j=1,…,K\theta_j,j=1,\dots, Kθj​,j=1,…,K is the parameters for the jjj-th component.

By the Bayes' law, the probability of XXX can be expressed as

P(x)=∑j=1KP(x∣Zj=1)P(Zj=1),\mathbb{P}(x)=\sum_{j=1}^K\mathbb{P}(x|Z_j=1)\mathbb{P}(Z_j=1),P(x)=j=1∑K​P(x∣Zj​=1)P(Zj​=1),

where P(x∣Zj=1)\mathbb{P}(x|Z_j=1)P(x∣Zj​=1) is the probability of the XXX when it comes from the jjj-th component. If we assume that all the components have a distribution p(x,θ)p(x,\theta)p(x,θ), this leads to the probability distribution for mixture model:

p(x)=∑j=1Kπjp(x,θj),p(x)= \sum_{j=1}^K\pi_jp(x,\theta_j),p(x)=j=1∑K​πj​p(x,θj​),

where πj=P(Zj=1)\pi_j=\mathbb{P}(Z_j=1)πj​=P(Zj​=1), ∑j=1Kπj=1\sum_{j=1}^K\pi_j=1∑j=1K​πj​=1, and θj\theta_jθj​. The multivariated case can be defined similarly.

Expectation and Maximization Algorithm#

The expectation and maximization (EM) algorithm can be used to learn the parameters of the mixture model. It works well especially for normal distributions. Suppose θj\theta_jθj​ stands for the parameters of the jjj-th components, then the parameters of mixture models are

{πj,θj}j=1,…,K.\lbrace \pi_j,\theta_j\rbrace\quad j = 1, \dots, K.{πj​,θj​}j=1,…,K.

Given the samples Xj,j=1,…,NX_j,j=1,\dots,NXj​,j=1,…,N, the latent random vector associated to the iii-th samples is denoted as Zi\boldsymbol{Z}_iZi​ where i=1,…,Ni=1,\dots, Ni=1,…,N. And let zijz_{ij}zij​ be the jjj-th component of zi\boldsymbol{z}_izi​ and there is only one component of zi\boldsymbol{z}_izi​ is 1 and rest must be vanish by the definition. Then we have

Gaussian Mixture#

One widely used distribution for the mixture model is a normal distribution p(x,θ)=N(x,μ,Σ)p(\boldsymbol{x},\boldsymbol{\theta})=N(\boldsymbol{x},\boldsymbol{\mu},\Sigma)p(x,θ)=N(x,μ,Σ). In this case, the EM algorithm can be further simplified due the maximum estimation of the mean and the variance of a normal distribution is well defined.

Let's define

pij(m)=p(zij=1∣xi,πj(m),μi(m),Σi(m))=πj(m)N(xi,μj(m),Σj(m))∑j=1Kπj(m)N(xi,μj(m),Σj(m))p_{ij}^{(m)} = p\left(z_{ij}=1|x_i, \pi^{(m)}_j,\boldsymbol{\mu}_i^{(m)},\Sigma_i^{(m)}\right)=\frac{\pi_j^{(m)}N\left(\boldsymbol{x}_i,\boldsymbol{\mu}_j^{(m)},\Sigma_j^{(m)}\right)}{\sum_{j=1}^K\pi_j^{(m)}N\left(\boldsymbol{x}_i,\boldsymbol{\mu}_j^{(m)},\Sigma_j^{(m)}\right)}pij(m)​=p(zij​=1∣xi​,πj(m)​,μi(m)​,Σi(m)​)=∑j=1K​πj(m)​N(xi​,μj(m)​,Σj(m)​)πj(m)​N(xi​,μj(m)​,Σj(m)​)​

With this function, the estimations at the m+1m+1m+1 step based on the mmm-th iterations can be expressed as
