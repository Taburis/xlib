Bernoulli Experiment#

A Bernoulli experiment is a class of statistical experiment that only 2 possible mutually exclusive outcome at a fixed probability for each trail. The random variable XXX for this experiment is formulated as a Bernoulli distribution p(x)p(x)p(x) that

p(x)=px(1−p)1−p,x=0,1.p(x)=p^x(1-p)^{1-p},\quad x=0,1.p(x)=px(1−p)1−p,x=0,1.

The ppp is the probability to get the outcome represented by 111. For XXX, it is easy to verify that E(X)=p\mathbb{E}(X)=pE(X)=p, Var(X)=p(1−p)\text{Var}(X)=p(1-p)Var(X)=p(1−p).

A random variable represents the outcome of a sequence of nnn Bernoulli trials forms the Binomial distribution b(n,p)b(n,p)b(n,p) where ppp is the probability to get 1 for each trail. The PMF f(x)f(x)f(x) is

f(x)=(nx)px(1−p)n−x,(n,x)=n!x!(n−x)!.f(x)=\begin{pmatrix}n \\ x\end{pmatrix}p^{x}(1-p)^{n-x}, \quad \begin{pmatrix}n,x\end{pmatrix}=\frac{n!}{x!(n-x)!}.f(x)=(nx​)px(1−p)n−x,(n,x​)=x!(n−x)!n!​.

where x=0,1,…,nx=0,1,\dots, nx=0,1,…,n.

  • The moment generate function for binomial distribution is M(t)=[(1−p)+pet]nM(t)=[(1-p)+pe^t]^nM(t)=[(1−p)+pet]n.
  • E(X)=np\mathbb{E}(X)=npE(X)=np and Var(X)=np(1−p)\text{Var}(X)=np(1-p)Var(X)=np(1−p).

Beta Distribution#

A beta distribution B(α,β)B(\alpha,\beta)B(α,β) is defined as the PDF:

f(x;α,β)=1B(α,β)xα−1(1−x)β−1=Γ(α+β)Γ(α)Γ(β)xα−1(1−x)β−1.f(x;\alpha,\beta)= \frac{1}{B(\alpha,\beta)}x^{\alpha-1}(1-x)^{\beta-1}=\frac{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}x^{\alpha-1}(1-x)^{\beta-1}.f(x;α,β)=B(α,β)1​xα−1(1−x)β−1=Γ(α)Γ(β)Γ(α+β)​xα−1(1−x)β−1.
