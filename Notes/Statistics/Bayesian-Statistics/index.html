Bayesian Statistics

Given a probability space (Ω,R,P)(\Omega, \mathcal{R},\mathbb{P})(Ω,R,P), the Bayes' theorem should the conditional probability follows the equation

P(A∣B)=P(A∪B)B,\mathbb{P}(A|B)= \frac{\mathbb{P}(A\cup B)}{\mathbb{B}},P(A∣B)=BP(A∪B)​,

Let XXX be a random variable with PDF f(x,θ)f(x,\theta)f(x,θ) where θ\thetaθ is a parameter unknow. The θ\thetaθ is assumed following a prior probability with PDF h(θ)h(\theta)h(θ).
