Sufficiency and Information

Given a sample {X}n\lbrace X\rbrace_n{X}n​ draw from a distribution fθf_\thetafθ​ where θ∈Θ\theta\in \Thetaθ∈Θ is some parameters unknown. A statistics T=T(X)T=T(\boldsymbol{X})T=T(X) is the called sufficient if the conditional probability P(X∣T=t)\mathbb{P}(\boldsymbol{X}|T=t)P(X∣T=t) is independent on the θ\thetaθ. It means that once the TTT is given, there's no other statistics, say H=H(X)H=H(\boldsymbol{X})H=H(X), can be used to determine the θ\thetaθ since P(H=h∣T=t)=P(Xh∣T=t)\mathbb{P}(H=h|T=t)=\mathbb{P}(\boldsymbol{X}_h|T=t)P(H=h∣T=t)=P(Xh​∣T=t) has no dependence on the θ\thetaθ, where H(Xh)=hH(\boldsymbol{X}_h)=hH(Xh​)=h. Suppose the distribution of TTT is gθg_\thetagθ​ and follows the Bayesian's formula, the definition of TTT is sufficient statistics if

P(X=x,T=t)P(T=t)→fθ(x)gθ(t)=H(x1,…,xn),t=T(x)=T(x1,…,xn),\frac{\mathbb{P}(\boldsymbol{X}=\boldsymbol{x}, T=t)}{\mathbb{P}(T=t)}\to \frac{f_\theta(\boldsymbol{x})}{g_\theta(t)}=H(x_1,\dots, x_n),\quad t=T(\boldsymbol{x})=T(x_1,\dots, x_n),P(T=t)P(X=x,T=t)​→gθ​(t)fθ​(x)​=H(x1​,…,xn​),t=T(x)=T(x1​,…,xn​),

where H(x)H(\boldsymbol{x})H(x) is an arbitrary function independent on the θ\thetaθ.

The Role of Sufficient Statistics#

Although we know that the sufficient statistics carried all the information about θ\thetaθ, the follow theorems detailed the role of the sufficient statistics in estimator.

Rao-Black Theorem:

Suppose X1,…,XnX_1,\dots,X_nX1​,…,Xn​ denote a sequence of iid random samples from a distribution fθf_\thetafθ​, where θ\thetaθ is unknown, and T=T(X)T=T(\boldsymbol{X})T=T(X) is a sufficient statistics for θ\thetaθ, and Y=Y(X)Y=Y(\boldsymbol{X})Y=Y(X) is an unbaised estimator of θ\thetaθ, then the statistics defined following

φ(T)=E(Y∣T)\varphi(T) = \mathbb{E}(Y|T)φ(T)=E(Y∣T)

is an unbiased estimator of θ\thetaθ with the variance no larger than that of YYY.

Proof: This follows from the inequality for two random variable X,YX,YX,Y that

E(X)=E[E(X∣Y)],\mathbb{E}(X)=\mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}(X|Y)],E(X)=E[E(X∣Y)],

and

Var(X)≥Var[E(X∣Y)].\text{Var}(X)\ge \text{Var}[\mathbb{E}(X|Y)].Var(X)≥Var[E(X∣Y)].

Let X=YX=YX=Y and Y=TY=TY=T the conclusion follows immediately.

The Rao-Black theorem tells us that if a minimum variance unbiased estimation (MVUE) for θ\thetaθ exists, then it can be a function of sufficient statistics. Because that if YYY is an MVUE of θ\thetaθ, then E(Y∣T)\mathbb{E}(Y|T)E(Y∣T) is a unbiased estimator of θ\thetaθ no worse than the YYY. Practically, we can restrict the searching of MVUE to the functions of a sufficient statistics. On the other hand, the sufficient statistics connected with the maximum likelihood estimator (MLE) by the following theorem:

Theorem:

Suppose TTT is a sufficient statistics for θ\thetaθ and a maximum likelihood estimator θ^\hat\thetaθ^ of θ\thetaθ exists uniquely, then θ^=θ(T)\hat\theta=\theta(T)θ^=θ(T), a function of TTT.

Proof: By the definition of the sufficiency, the likelihood function can be epxressed

L(θ,x1,…,xn)=∏ifθ(xi)=gθ(t)H(x1,…,xn),L(\theta, x_1,\dots, x_n)=\prod_if_\theta(x_i)=g_\theta(t)H(x_1,\dots, x_n),L(θ,x1​,…,xn​)=i∏​fθ​(xi​)=gθ​(t)H(x1​,…,xn​),

and the MLE θ^\hat\thetaθ^ maximizing the LLL will maximize the gθ(t)g_\theta(t)gθ​(t) as well since no θ\thetaθ dependent in HHH. So that it must be a function of TTT alone.

We know the MLEs are asymptotic unbiased estimators for θ\thetaθ, so we usually obtain a unbiased estimator from sufficient statistics by the procedure:

  1. Found a sufficient statistics TTT for θ\thetaθ.
  2. Obtain the MLE θ^=θ^(T)\hat\theta=\hat\theta(T)θ^=θ^(T) by the equation ∂ℓ(θ)/∂θ=0\partial\ell(\theta)/\partial\theta=0∂ℓ(θ)/∂θ=0.
  3. Calculate the E(θ^)\mathbb{E}(\hat\theta)E(θ^) and express the θ=θ(T)\theta=\theta(T)θ=θ(T) to obtain the unbiased estimator.

Completeness#

Let ZZZ be a random variable with a PDF or PMF as one member of the family functions {h(z,θ):θ∈Θ}\lbrace h(z,\theta):\theta\in\Theta\rbrace{h(z,θ):θ∈Θ}. It is called complete family if E(φ(z))=0\mathbb{E}(\varphi(z))=0E(φ(z))=0 implies that φ(z)=0\varphi(z)=0φ(z)=0 except on those points with probability equal 0.

The completeness of the distributions ensure that if there are two unbiased estimator for θ\thetaθ, say φ(T),ψ(T)\varphi(T), \psi(T)φ(T),ψ(T), as function of a sufficient statistics TTT, then φ(T)=ψ(T)\varphi(T)=\psi(T)φ(T)=ψ(T) except some points with probability 0. A complete sufficient statistics is a sufficient statistics with a distribution belong to a complete family.

Lehmann and Scheffe:

Let X1,…,XnX_1,\dots, X_nX1​,…,Xn​ be a random sample from a distribution fθ,θ∈Θf_\theta,\theta\in\Thetafθ​,θ∈Θ, and TTT is a sufficient statistics with distribution belong to a complete family gθ(T)g_\theta(T)gθ​(T) (complete sufficient statistics). If there is a function of TTT, φ(T)\varphi(T)φ(T), is an unbiased estimator of θ\thetaθ, then it is unique MVUE of θ\thetaθ.

Proof: The uniqueness follows from the discussion above. To show the minimum variance property, noticed that if there is any other unbiased estimator as functions of XiX_iXi​, there must be an unbiased estimator as function of TTT with no larger variance based on the Rho-Blackwell's theorem. The uniqueness of this estimator implies that it is MVUE for θ\thetaθ.

Exponentail Class of Distributions#

A family of the distributions with the following form is called the exponential class:

f(x,θ)=exp⁡{p(θ)K(x)+H(x)+q(θ)},x∈Sf(x,\theta) = \exp\left\lbrace p(\theta)K(x)+H(x)+q(\theta)\right\rbrace,\quad x\in Sf(x,θ)=exp{p(θ)K(x)+H(x)+q(θ)},x∈S

where SSS is the support of XXX. Furthermore, a exponential class is regular if it satisfies the following conditions:

  1. SSS, the support of XXX, doesn't depends on θ\thetaθ.
  2. p(θ)p(\theta)p(θ) is a non-trivial continuous function of θ∈Θ\theta\in\Thetaθ∈Θ.
  3. This condition is a little difference for discrete and continuous cases:
    • If XXX is continuous, then each of K(x)K(x)K(x) shouldn't be a constant and H(x)H(x)H(x) is a conitnuous function of x∈Sx\in Sx∈S.
    • If XXX is discrete, then K(x)K(x)K(x) has to be a non-trivial function of x∈Sx\in Sx∈S.

    fT(t,θ)=R(t)exp⁡{p(θ)t+nq(θ)},t∈ST,f_T(t, \theta)=R(t)\exp\lbrace p(\theta)t+nq(\theta)\rbrace,\quad t\in S_T,fT​(t,θ)=R(t)exp{p(θ)t+nq(θ)},t∈ST​,
    where STS_TST​ is the support of TTT. Both STS_TST​ and R(T)R(T)R(T) is independent on θ\thetaθ.
